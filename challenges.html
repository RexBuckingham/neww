Challenges of Urban Food Security

  • Higher food prices due to transportation costs, less competition among retailers, and higher land prices.
  • Food deserts: areas lacking access to affordable and nutritious food, with few or no grocery stores, farmers' markets, or fresh produce options.
  • Low wages, unemployment, and underemployment contributing to financial instability and food insecurity.
  • Income inequality limiting access to healthy food for some residents.
  • Lack of knowledge about healthy eating habits, meal planning, and cooking skills, leading to reliance on processed foods.
  • Limited access to fresh fruits and vegetables in some grocery stores.
  • Food waste: large quantities of food discarded by households, restaurants, and retailers, representing lost resources and environmental problems.
